## 46. What did your database design make easy, and what was difficult?

### What was easy:

The relational design made it easy to connect information between students, programs, instructors, attendance, and payments.

Using foreign keys and JOIN operations allowed management reports to be created quickly without duplicate data.

Examples:
- Finding students in each program.
- Calculating revenue.
- Tracking attendance.
- Identifying risks.

### What was difficult:

The most difficult part was designing relationships correctly and deciding which information belongs in each table.

Handling missing payments and calculating attendance rates required careful SQL logic because the information comes from multiple tables.

Overall, normalization made the database more reliable and easier to maintain.
